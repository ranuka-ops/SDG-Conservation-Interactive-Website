Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs)

AUVs are revolutionizing how we map the seafloor and monitor deep-sea ecosystems. These untethered robots can dive deeper and stay submerged longer than human divers, collecting high-resolution oceanographic data without putting humans at risk.

AI and Machine Learning in Reef Monitoring

Artificial Intelligence is being trained to analyze thousands of underwater photographs in minutes. By identifying coral bleaching and specific fish species, AI helps marine biologists track the health of ecosystems with unprecedented speed and accuracy.

3D Printed Coral Reefs

To combat habitat loss, conservationists are deploying 3D-printed structures made of sustainable materials like terracotta. These artificial reefs provide a textured foundation for coral polyps to attach to and grow, accelerating reef restoration.

Satellite Tracking & Big Data

Satellites are now used to track the movements of endangered marine megafauna like sharks and whales. Furthermore, satellite data helps authorities detect illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing vessels via their communication signals.
